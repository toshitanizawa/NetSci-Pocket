In 1999, Laszlo Barabasi and Reka Albert proposed a new algorithm for generating a growing network. When a person joins to a social group, he probably would not make friends randomly. He would rather got acquaintance with a social person who has a lot of frieands. The algorithm proposed by Barabasi and Albert incorporates this tendency and the networks generated by the algorithm actually have a part of the properties that the networks of human relationship in the real world.

Step 1
Prepare an initial network as a seed.

Step 2
Next add a new node to the initial network and connect the node to two randomly chosen nodes in the initial network.

Step3
Add one more node to the existing network. In making connection from the new node to existing nodes, the nodes of larger degree are likely to be connected to the newly added node. This tendency in making connection is called the "preferential attachment".

Step 4
Repeat Step 3 until the total node number reaches a given value.
